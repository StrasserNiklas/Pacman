Pac-Man is a maze arcade game developed and released by Namco in 1980. The original Japanese title of Puck Man was changed to Pac-Man for international releases as a preventative measure against defacement of the arcade machines. The player controls Pac-Man, who must eat all the dots inside an enclosed maze while avoiding four colored ghosts. Eating large flashing dots called power pellets causes the ghosts to turn blue, allowing Pac-Man to eat them for bonus points. It is the first game to run on the Namco Pac-Man arcade board.

Instructions:

The game ends when you eat all the food items or you have 0 lives left. You can control the Pacman with the arrow keys. If you are on mobile, press the "Toggle mobile controls" button on the game page to show touch keys.

Element Description

You will control this Pacman with the arrow keys.

You'll lose a life and 20 points if you hit a ghost.

The ghosts randomly decide their direction when they encounter an intersection/junction.

If you eat the food item, your points increase by 5.

If you eat the power up, the ghosts will get scared and you can eat them to gain 100 points for a short period of time.

Scared ghost. A ghost will get scared if he hits the Pacman or a power up is eaten by the Pacman.

If you eat a ghost will it is scared because of the powerup, it will be dead for a short time and unable to be eaten.

If you move into the portal, you will be teleported to a random location with food. Ghosts will teleport to the second portal.
